ABOUT

FORWARD/S

Introducing Forward/s by Burberry, where individualism and modernism converge in an extraordinary fashion fusion. Our journey began with a collective brainstorm, pondering how to align Burberry with the core values of streetwear.

Delving into Burberry's iconic tan hues, symbolic of a bygone era of collectivism and patriotism, we recognized the need to infuse our brand with vibrant, captivating colors and patterns that resonate with streetwear's penchant for uniqueness.

Drawing inspiration from the functional origins of the original Burberry trench coats, designed to brave the harshest British weather, we observed a kinship with techwear—a contemporary clothing style characterized by sleek black aesthetics, advanced materials, and integrated technology. Our aim was to pay homage to Burberry's heritage while embracing the cutting-edge fashion of today.

Enter Forward/s by Burberry, a groundbreaking collection that combines hyper-functional techwear with an audacious interplay of captivating colors and patterns. This avant-garde fusion ensures that Forward/s boldly stands out amidst the crowd, capturing the essence of modern culture and becoming an indispensable choice for streetwear aficionados.

OUR HISTORY

The history of techwear can be traced back to the early 20th century when advancements in textile technology and manufacturing techniques paved the way for the development of functional and innovative clothing. However, it wasn't until the late 20th century and early 21st century that techwear as a distinct fashion style began to emerge.

In the 1980s and 1990s, outdoor apparel brands like The North Face and Patagonia introduced performance-focused clothing that incorporated advanced materials such as Gore-Tex, which offered waterproofing and breathability. These garments were primarily designed for outdoor activities like hiking, mountaineering, and skiing.

The rise of urban streetwear in the late 1990s and early 2000s had a significant influence on the development of techwear. Streetwear brands like ACRONYM, Stone Island, and Y-3 (a collaboration between Yohji Yamamoto and Adidas) started incorporating technical fabrics, innovative design elements, and futuristic aesthetics into their collections. This fusion of fashion and functionality laid the foundation for the techwear style.

The term "techwear" itself gained prominence in the 2010s, as online communities and fashion enthusiasts began to use it to describe this emerging fashion trend. The rise of social media platforms and online forums provided a platform for individuals to share and discuss their interest in techwear, leading to increased visibility and popularity.

Techwear's aesthetic and functional principles were heavily influenced by science fiction, cyberpunk literature, and dystopian visions of the future. The visual aesthetics of films like "Blade Runner" and "The Matrix" played a significant role in shaping the futuristic and utilitarian design elements of techwear.

As the popularity of techwear grew, designers and brands started to push the boundaries of what was considered possible in clothing design. They began incorporating features such as modular components, hidden pockets, and integrated technology into their garments. This emphasis on innovation and functionality further solidified techwear as a distinct fashion category.

Today, techwear continues to evolve and expand, with designers exploring new materials, incorporating smart textiles, and experimenting with cutting-edge technologies. The style has garnered a dedicated following and has made its way into mainstream fashion, influencing not only streetwear but also high-end designer collections.

Overall, the history of techwear is a story of the intersection between fashion, technology, and functionality. It has evolved from humble beginnings in performance outdoor apparel to a fashion movement that blends futuristic aesthetics with advanced materials and design innovation.
